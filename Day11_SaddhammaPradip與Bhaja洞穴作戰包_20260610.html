🔴 Anti-Pity 原則（全日最高優先）

Lokamitra 親筆否定「重擔」框架： 「IT WAS NOT A BURDEN. I felt myself so blessed, so fortunate.」 「please do not go on about burden/weight. The work was incredibly demanding but even more of a joy」

今天在任何提問中，禁止將早期 SP 條件框架為「苦難」「艱辛」「重擔」。作者自己的定位是：條件嚴苛，但他感到蒙福與幸運。提問時用「challenging」「demanding」，絕不用「burden」「suffering」「hardship」。

1.1 初見 Bhaja（1978 年 10 月）

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH08 L678-692）**： > Soon after arriving in Pune, in October 1978, I had visited the Bhaja caves with two friends. The caves are cut into the almost vertical side of a hill, the top of which was an ancient fort, Visapur, where there were also Buddhist caves. Apparently there were Buddhist caves near all the main forts in Maharashtra, perhaps because both needed water all year round, although there must have been other reasons. On top of the hill about a kilometre in front of Bhaja caves was another majestic fort, Lohagad, which also had some Buddhist caves at the very top as we found out when we went there a couple of years later. There were other caves in vicinity. Clearly it was an important place for Buddhist practice 2,000 years ago. That day in October 1978, I looked out straight in front of us, where the steep incline that rose to the fort, shallowed out to join the valley floor. I voiced aloud how good it would be to have a retreat centre there, so that we could look up into the caves as we were practicing. We became more familiar with the caves through our day retreats there in 1979 and 1980. **翻譯**： 1978 年 10 月，剛到浦那不久，我和兩個朋友去看了巴賈石窟。石窟嵌在幾乎垂直的山壁上，山頂是古城維薩普爾，那裡也有佛教石窟。馬哈拉施特拉邦所有主要城堡附近似乎都有佛教石窟，也許因為兩者都需要全年不斷的水源，不過肯定還有別的原因。巴賈石窟前方大約一公里處的山頂上，另一座雄偉的羅哈加德城堡，頂部也有佛教石窟——我們幾年後爬上去才發現。附近還有其他石窟。兩千年前這裡顯然是重要的佛教修行地。1978 年 10 月那天，我望向正前方，陡峭的山坡在那裡漸緩，與谷底相接。我脫口說出，要是能在那裡建一座禪修中心就好了，這樣修行的時候就可以仰望石窟。1979 和 1980 年我們在那裡辦了日間靜修，對石窟越來越熟悉。 **田野對應**：站在 SP 校園往上看，應該能看到這個視角——石窟在上方，校園在谷底。今天反覆對照這個空間感。

1.2 Buddhapriya 找到土地（1981 年底）

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH08 L695-710）**： > Late in 1981 one of our new members of the Order, Buddhapriya, who came from a small and remote village above Bhaja caves, found land just where we had looked out upon in 1978, a total coincidence. The process of legally transferring 5 acres of land took over a year but that was not long by local standards and it was well worth it. In January 1983 we laid the foundation stone and quickly drew up plans for the first building. The land bordered on the government forest boundary which extended all the way up to Lohagad fort, which meant no one could build there, although we could walk there. In other words we had a huge back garden of hundreds of acres to walk in. Our first problem was finding water – there was very little in the valley even though we were under a hill, and the monsoon rains were very heavy there. We were told by the water finder that there was an 80% run off of rain water, very little staying in the land. **翻譯**： 1981 年底，我們的新法行者覺悅，他來自巴賈石窟上方一個偏遠小村，找到了一塊地——正好是我們 1978 年望出去的那個位置，完全巧合。五英畝土地的合法轉移程序花了一年多，但以當地標準來說不算久，而且值得。1983 年 1 月我們安了奠基石，很快畫了第一棟建築的設計圖。土地緊鄰政府林區邊界，一路延伸到羅哈加德城堡——意思是那邊不能蓋房子，但我們可以走過去。換句話說，我們有幾百英畝的後花園可以散步。第一個問題是找水——谷裡的水很少，雖然我們在山腳下，而且雨季的雨量很大。找水的人告訴我們，百分之八十的雨水都流走了，留在地裡的很少。 **田野對應**：⚠️ 注意原文說「a total coincidence」，但 Feedback Matrix 明確指出 Buddhapriya 是在 Lokamitra 建議他去看那個山谷之後才找到的，並非完全偶然。今天必須核實這個細節。

1.3 早期建設：沒有路，頭上頂建材（1983 年前後）

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH08 L712-720）**： > There was no road to the centre although in the dry season when the crops had been taken up, the building materials could be carried over the fields by bullock cart. In the monsoon and for some months after this was not possible because the fields were full of rice. We took as much as we could before the rains, and after that had no alternative but to carry them on our heads for the 30 minutes' walk from the village along the narrow slippery pathways in between the rice fields. We held some working retreats to do this and help with some building work, which I enthusiastically joined in, rather too enthusiastically as I hurt my back rather badly. **翻譯**： 中心沒有路可以通——雖然在旱季莊稼收成之後，建材可以用牛車從田裡運過去。雨季和之後幾個月就不行了，因為田裡全是稻子。我們在雨季前盡量多運，雨季後就只剩一個辦法：從村莊出發，沿著稻田间窄窄滑滑的小路走三十分鐘，把建材頂在頭上搬過去。我們辦了幾次勞動靜修，幫忙搬建材和蓋房子，我也很起勁地參加——結果太起勁了，傷了腰，傷得不輕。 **田野對應**：今天走從 Malavali 火車站到 SP 的路，觀察是否仍需經過稻田小路。注意 Lokamitra 自己也搬了，而且傷了腰——這不是「受苦」，是「投入」。

He was extremely pleased about the location, connecting us with early Buddhists in India. The opening was followed by a week's ordination retreat, at the end of which we had 8 new members of our Order, this time not only from Pune but also Bombay. Ulhasnagar and Aurangabad. **翻譯**： 1983 年 12 月，僧護主持了開幕，並為中心命名「妙法光」（Sadhamma Pradip，真實佛法的燈），取自安貝卡博士的《佛陀及其佛法》中「妙法」一章。他對這個地點非常滿意，認為把我們和印度的早期佛教徒連在了一起。開幕之後是一週的授戒靜修，結束時有八位新法行者加入，這次不只來自浦那，還有孟買、烏拉斯納格爾和奧蘭加巴德。 **田野對應**：今天找奠基石或紀念牌。名稱來源是安貝卡的書，不是隨便取的。

1.5 ⚠️ Kondhanpur 芒果林與七條眼鏡蛇（1981 年獨修，非 SP）

📖 展開原文與翻譯（🔴 本日最重要修正） **原文（CH08 L731-760）**： > We had hoped the new centre would be ready for the one month ordination preparation retreat we held earlier in May that year, but it was not. Instead we used a mango grove at Kondhanpur, in an alluring valley behind the majestic Sinhagad (Lion) fort a little south of Pune, in which there were three extremely small and run down huts, constructed by the owner for meditation, although I do not think he had ever used them. In fact I had often looked down the valley when we passed it on the main road in the bus to and fro Pune, thinking what an ideal area it seemed for a retreat cente. When I was looking for a place to have a one month solitary retreat towards the end of the monsoon in 1981 two friends found this grove in the valley, without me suggesting they look there. During that solitary retreat I saw 7 cobras (nagas), auspicious signs for spiritual practice. So I was very familiar with the grove. The retreat in May 1983 was at the height of the summer. There was not space for all 23 participants to sleep in the huts and most slept outside, which was fine in the very hot weather. We also ate, studied the Dharma and meditated outside. Water had to be collected from the well 400 metres away, and stored it in drums at the retreat. Around the well was a large mango grove with the most delicious fruit. We would rise at 4 or 4.30 in the morning to bring water, retreatants eagerly rushing to get to the well first so they could collect any mangoes that had fallen in the night. Although it was very basic with no toilets, water or electricity all participants deepened their study and meditation, and their communication with fellows on the path. From then on, while I was involved in ordination preparation for next 12 years, I made a criterion for ordination participation in a one month retreat, besides the other shorter training retreats. **翻譯**： 我們原本希望新中心能趕上那年五月的一個月授戒準備靜修，但沒來得及。我們改用孔德布爾的一處芒果林——在浦那南方宏偉的辛哈加德（獅子）城堡背後一處迷人的山谷裡。那裡有三間極小極破的茅屋，是地主蓋來打坐的，不過我想他從沒用過。事實上，我們搭巴士往返浦那時，我經常望向那個山谷，心想那裡真是靜修的理想地點。1981 年雨季尾聲，我想找個地方做一個月的獨修，兩位朋友在山谷裡找到了這片芒果林——不是我建議他們去找的。在那次獨修中，我看見了七條眼鏡蛇（nagas），是修行的吉兆。所以我對那片芒果林非常熟悉。1983 年五月的靜修趕上了最熱的季節。二十三個人，茅屋睡不下，大多數人睡在戶外，天氣很熱，倒也無妨。吃飯、學法、打坐都在戶外。水要從四百米外的井裡打回來，存在靜修處的桶裡。井邊有一大片芒果林，果實極甜。我們清晨四點或四點半起床打水，靜修者爭著搶先到井邊，好撿夜裡掉落的芒果。雖然條件極為基本——沒有廁所、沒有自來水、沒有電——所有參與者都加深了對法和禪修的理解，也加深了同行者之間的交流。從那以後，在我參與授戒準備的十二年裡，我把參加一個月靜修列為授戒的必要條件，此外還有其他較短的訓練靜修。 **田野對應**：🔴🔴🔴 **七條眼鏡蛇發生在 Kondhanpur（1981 年獨修），不在 Saddhamma Pradip。** 舊版 Q4 將此錯誤放在 SP，必須修正。Kondhanpur 是另一個地點，在 Sinhagad 城堡背後，距浦那南方。今天在 SP 不需要問眼鏡蛇——那是 Kondhanpur 的故事。但可以在 Bhaja 問 Lokamitra 是否在 Bhaja 附近也見過蛇。

1.6 種姓歧視：水龍頭前的排隊

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH08 L774-790）**： > Even in our own retreat centre we could not get away from caste. Every May the wells in the surrounding villages would dry up. Some people from one nearby village would take their water pots by train to the next town and fill them there. We had the only water pump for miles around, such was terrible state of the land. In the mornings and evenings up to 200 villagers would tramp across the fields, with their water pots on their heads, to queue up for water from our pump. I knew some of the local Buddhists who had worked on our buildings. One day I noticed that even after an hour in the queue the local Buddhists were in the same position at the back. Enquiring what was happening I was told that the caste Hindus insisted they had priority through their caste status, even though they came later than the Buddhists. I remedied this by putting a couple of Buddhist in charge of the queue. The audaciousness of the caste Hindus at our Buddhist retreat centre shocked me and once again underlined how deeply caste was ingrained in people's attitudes, including many Buddhists. **翻譯**： 即使在我們自己的靜修中心，也躲不過種姓。每年五月，周圍村莊的水井都會乾涸。附近一個村的人會搭火車到下一個鎮去裝水。方圓幾英里內只有我們這裡有水泵——土地的狀況就是這麼惡劣。早晚兩頭，最多兩百個村民頂著水壺穿過田野，排隊從我們的水泵取水。我認識一些當地佛教徒，他們幫我們蓋過房子。有一天我注意到，當地佛教徒排了一個小時的隊，位置還是在最後面。我問了才知道，種姓印度教徒堅持他們的種姓地位讓他們有優先權，即便他們比佛教徒晚來。我的辦法是讓幾個佛教徒負責管排隊。種姓印度教徒在我們的佛教靜修中心如此理直氣壯，讓我震驚，也再一次印證了種姓觀念在人們心中有多深——包括許多佛教徒。 **田野對應**：今天觀察 SP 是否仍有水泵？周圍村莊的水源狀況？這段的重點不是「苦難」，而是種姓如何滲透到看起來與世隔絕的空間裡。

1.7 聖地景觀與 SP 的生活

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH08 L793-825）**： > Over the years Sadhamma Pradip has developed into a large and beautiful retreat centre, with many trees, and has introduced thousands of people to the magic of the Dharma, as well as helping those who wanted to take their practice more deeply. It has been a continual source of inspiration to have the Bhaja caves just a few hundred metres away from us. > In the evening the stupa in the main Bhaja cave lights up as the setting sun hits it. It was my favourite place in Maharashtra for many years, with the caves opposite us and the two forts above and across the other side of the valley, the outline of Karla caves. Not only did we live in beauty, but we connected with ancient Buddhist heritage. For a few years I would spend up to 10 weeks there annually leading retreats. For the first years there was no electricity and so no lights or fans, and no phone, but we managed well, perhaps better as the lack of these increased our sense of simplicity and remoteness from the world. In the monsoon the rains were incessant much of the time and many waterfalls gushed down from the hills above in their white fury. Sometimes the rains were so heavy that all the fields from us to the railway line about 2 kilometres away was just one continuous sheet of water, with trees and the occasional cottage rising above it. The most enjoyable part of the year was September as the rains were receding, the air was fresh and clear, and the fields in front of us and forest behind us were a medley of different greens. Then we would take our daily bath in the fast-flowing streams behind the retreat centre. Behind us the hills were covered in ancient trees and thick undergrowth. I used to take people for walks so that we could talk more. Later I understood that people were frightened to come with me because there of the many snakes (I saw a few), wild pigs and panthers that roamed the hills. **翻譯**： 多年來，妙法光發展成一座大型而美麗的靜修中心，種了許多樹，引導了數千人接觸佛法的魔力，也幫助了那些想深入修行的人。巴賈石窟就在幾百米外，一直是持續的靈感來源。 傍晚，夕陽照進巴賈主窟的佛塔，佛塔會亮起來。這裡多年來是我在馬哈拉施特拉最喜歡的地方——對面是石窟，上方和谷的對岸是兩座城堡，遠處是卡爾拉石窟的輪廓。我們不只住在美裡，我們和古老的佛教遺產連在了一起。有幾年我每年在那裡帶靜修長達十週。最初幾年沒有電，所以沒有燈和風扇，也沒有電話，但我們過得很好——也許更好，因為這些東西的缺席加深了我們的簡樸感和與世隔絕感。雨季時雨幾乎不停，許多瀑布從上方山丘傾瀉而下，白花花地怒吼。有時雨大到從我們這裡到大約兩公里外的鐵路線之間的田地，變成一片連續的水面，只有樹木和偶爾的茅屋露出。一年中最愉快的時光是九月，雨漸漸停了，空氣清新透亮，我們前面的田野和後面的森林是各種不同層次的綠。那時我們會到中心後方水流湍急的溪裡洗澡。我們後方的山丘覆蓋著古樹和濃密的灌木。我以前會帶人散步，這樣可以多聊一些。後來我才明白，大家不敢跟我走，因為山上有許多蛇（我見過幾條）、野豬和黑豹出沒。 **田野對應**：這段是今天所有五感記錄的基準。注意 Lokamitra 的語調——他沒有在抱怨沒有電、沒有路、有蛇。他說「we managed well, perhaps better」。這是 Anti-Pity 原則最直接的文本依據。

1.8 Bhaja 靈性體驗的時間線修正

📖 展開原文與翻譯（🔴 Feedback Matrix S9-04-M-008） **Feedback Matrix 原始紀錄（S9-04-M-008）**： > | 編號 | 草稿原文摘要 | 作者回饋原話 | 模式 | > |:---|:---|:---|:---:| > | S9-04-M-008 | Bhaja 洞穴中「something inside him was called forth」 | "NO – I had had those deep experiences in Bhaja a year earlier" | M/F | **翻譯與解讀**： 作者明確否定草稿中「在 Ajanta 洞穴裡有深刻體驗」的敘述，並指出：**深刻的靈性體驗發生在 Bhaja，而且比 Ajanta 早一年**。 這意味著： 1. **Bhaja 的體驗是先發生的**，不是同時，更不是 Ajanta 之後 2. **Ajanta 的體驗不是 Lokamitra 的**——作者說「This is NOT my experience」 3. 今天在 Bhaja 時，所有關於靈性體驗的提問必須圍繞 **Bhaja**，而非 Ajanta

1.9 「不是重擔」——Anti-Pity 的文本依據

📖 展開原文與翻譯（🔴 全書最核心的敘事框架修正） **作者原話（CH04, S9-04-P-011）**： > "IT WAS NOT A BURDEN. I felt myself so blessed, so fortunate." > > "please do not go on about burden/weight. The work was incredibly demanding but even more of a joy" **翻譯**： 「那不是重擔。我覺得自己蒙受了祝福，如此幸運。」 「請不要一直說重擔、重量。這份工作極其吃力，但更是極大的喜悅。」 **田野對應**：今天所有關於早期條件的提問，禁止用「苦」「難」「重擔」的框架。正確的框架是：**吃力但蒙福**（demanding but blessed）。問「當時的條件是什麼樣？」而非「當時有多苦？」

1.10 Konkan 巡迴（1982 年 4 月，CH09）

📖 展開原文與翻譯 **原文（CH09 L1-50）**： > In April 1982, soon after Urgyen Sangharakshita's visit, I was taken on a tour of the central Konkan, the coastal region in between Mumbai and Goa. It was a very beautiful area, hilly with red laterite soil, famous for its fruit especially the alphonso mango, considered the best in India. Beautiful though it was, the area was very backward and poor, with much drunkenness, and of course the Dalits suffered most. Fortunately most Buddhist families had a relation working in Bombay, on whom they depended for survival. It was our friends in Bombay who came from villages in this area, who organised this and future tours, especially Bodhisen, who was our most active friend there. It had a special significance for followers of Dr. Ambedkar, being the area where his family had its ancestral roots, and where he spent considerable time in his youth. Mahad, where Dr. Ambedkar had drunk water from the common water tank, and burnt the Manusmriti, was at the centre of this area. > The tour consisted of two lectures a day in different villages, each village being a centre for 20 or so villages. The talks were always some distance apart, and some involved walking for some distance in the hot April sun due to an absence of roads. As people came from far and wide, talks were often delayed until after midnight; they were a special event for the villages concerned with a joyful festive atmosphere, and food being served to all. Konkani villages were usually spread out, and so the Buddhist or old Mahar section of the village was often like an separate village, instead of a locality of a larger. I spoke on different subjects including drunkenness which was rife in the area. Dr. Ambedkar had been emphatic about not drinking as so many of the poorest of his brethren destroyed themselves and their families in this way; I saw this myself on a number of occasions. Here, like other places where I touched on the subject, at the end of the talk I would invariably be approached by someone saying how much he appreciated my talk – they, themselves reeking of alcohol! **翻譯**： 1982 年 4 月，僧護訪問結束後不久，我被帶去巡迴康坎中部——孟買和果阿之間的海岸地區。那裡很美，丘陵起伏，紅色的紅土，以水果聞名，尤其是被認為印度最好的阿方索芒果。美歸美，這個地區卻非常落後貧窮，酗酒嚴重，達利特人受害最深。幸好大多數佛教家庭都有親戚在孟買工作，靠他們維生。組織這次和之後巡迴的，是那些從這一帶村莊來孟買的朋友，特別是菩提森，他是我們在那裡最活躍的朋友。這個地區對安貝卡博士的追隨者有特殊意義——他的家族根源在這裡，他年輕時也在這裡度過不少時間。馬哈德——安貝卡博士從公共水槽喝水和焚燒《摩奴法典》的地方——就在這個地區的中心。 巡迴是每天在兩個不同的村莊演講，每個村莊是大約二十個村莊的中心。演講場地之間總有段距離，有些要在四月烈日下走很遠，因為沒有路。人們從四面八方趕來，演講常常拖到午夜之後才開始；對這些村莊來說，這是特別的節日，歡樂的氣氛，所有人都有飯吃。康坎的村莊通常很分散，所以佛教徒或舊馬哈爾區往往像一個獨立的村莊，而不是更大聚落的一部分。我講了不同的主題，包括酗酒——這在當地很嚴重。安貝卡博士堅決反對飲酒，因為他那最貧困的同胞中有太多人這樣毀了自己和家庭；我親眼見過好幾次。在這裡，就像我在其他地方觸及這個主題時一樣，演講結束後總有人走過來說他多麼感激我的演講——而他們自己，滿身酒氣！ **田野對應**：Konkan 巡迴是 SP 建立前後的重要活動。今天不需要問 Konkan，但它是理解 Lokamitra 當時工作強度的背景——每天兩場演講，烈日下走路，半夜才開始。

第二部分：提問與組織

核心目標：一切提問都為了讓這本書寫得更好。每個問題都必須有明確的書稿對應或田野查證目的。

提問風格參考 Day 08 Vivekratna 包：口語英文短句、每題有中文概念、有引申追問。 所有提問遵守 Anti-Pity 原則，不用 burden/suffering/hardship 框架。

主題一：土地的發現與連結

Q1. Buddhapriya 是怎麼找到這塊地的？

[EN] I read that Buddhapriya found this land in 1981. He was from a village above Bhaja caves. Can you tell me the story? Did Lokamitra suggest he look in this valley, or did Buddhapriya find it on his own?

[TW] 我讀到覺悅在 1981 年找到了這塊地。他來自巴賈石窟上方的一個村莊。您能說說這個故事嗎？是世友建議他去這個山谷看看，還是他自己找到的？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** What kind of person was Buddhapriya? Was he a teacher before joining the Order? What made him look for land? - **[TW]** 覺悅是什麼樣的人？加入法團之前是老師嗎？是什麼讓他想去找地？ - **[EN]** I heard Lokamitra had visited Bhaja caves in 1978 and looked at this valley and said, "It would be good to have a retreat centre here." Is that right? - **[TW]** 我聽說世友 1978 年去巴賈石窟時，望著這個山谷說「在這裡建一座禪修中心就好了」。是這樣嗎？ - **[EN]** Did Buddhapriya play a role in Lokamitra's wedding? I read he stood in for the father. - **[TW]** 覺悅在世友的婚禮中扮演了角色嗎？我讀到他代替了父親的角色。

🔴 SSOT 查證：手稿寫「a total coincidence」，Feedback Matrix 寫「after Lokamitra had suggested they look up that particular valley」。今天必須釐清到底是巧合還是世友建議的。

Q2. 1978 年第一次看到這個山谷的時候，世友說了什麼？

[TW] 世友寫道，1978 年 10 月他站在這裡望向山谷時，脫口說出在這裡建一座禪修中心該有多好。您能想像那個時刻嗎？他之後常提起這件事嗎？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** Did he ever tell you what he felt when he first saw this place? - **[TW]** 他有沒有告訴過您他第一次看到這個地方時的感受？ - **[EN]** Was there a moment when he realized the dream was actually coming true? - **[TW]** 有沒有哪個時刻，他意識到夢想真的在實現了？

Q3. 奠基那天是什麼樣子？

[EN] The centre was inaugurated in December 1983 by Sangharakshita. He named it "Sadhamma Pradip" — light of the true Dhamma. What do you remember about that day? Who was there?

[TW] 中心在 1983 年 12 月由僧護主持開幕。他命名為「妙法光」——真實佛法的燈。您記得那天嗎？誰在場？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** Why did Sangharakshita choose that name from Dr. Ambedkar's book? - **[TW]** 僧護為什麼從安貝卡博士的書中選了這個名字？ - **[EN]** Eight new Order members joined after that ordination retreat. Were they from Pune, Bombay, and other places? - **[TW]** 授戒靜修後有八位新法行者加入。他們來自浦那、孟買和其他地方嗎？

主題二：條件與生活——吃力但蒙福

Q4. 最早來這裡的人，過的是什麼樣的日子？

[EN] In the early years there was no road, no electricity, no phone. Lokamitra wrote that they carried building materials on their heads for 30 minutes through the rice fields. What was daily life like?

[TW] 最初幾年沒有路、沒有電、沒有電話。世友寫他們把建材頂在頭上，走三十分鐘穿過稻田。日常是什麼樣的？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** Lokamitra said, "We managed well, perhaps better, as the lack of these increased our sense of simplicity." Did people actually enjoy the simplicity? - **[TW]** 世友說「我們過得很好，也許更好，因為這些東西的缺席加深了我們的簡樸感」。大家真的享受那種簡樸嗎？ - **[EN]** He also hurt his back carrying materials. Did that stop him from joining the work? - **[TW]** 他搬建材時還傷了腰。那有讓他停止參加勞動嗎？ - **[EN]** How did you get water in the early days? Was there a pump from the start? - **[TW]** 早期怎麼取水？一開始就有水泵嗎？

⚠️ Anti-Pity 提醒：問「什麼樣的日子」，不問「多苦」。Lokamitra 自己說「not a burden」，所以提問框架用「demanding」而非「hardship」。

Q5. 水龍頭前的種姓——您見過嗎？

[EN] Lokamitra wrote that up to 200 villagers would come to queue for water from the centre's pump. One day he noticed that Buddhist villagers were always at the back of the queue, even after waiting an hour, because caste Hindus insisted on their priority. He put Buddhists in charge of the queue. Is this still remembered here?

[TW] 世友寫過，最多兩百個村民會來中心的水泵排隊取水。有一天他注意到佛教徒排了一小時還在最後面，因為種姓印度教徒堅持他們有優先權。他讓佛教徒管排隊。這裡還有人記得這件事嗎？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** Is the water pump still here? Do villagers still come? - **[TW]** 水泵還在嗎？村民還會來嗎？ - **[EN]** Was caste conflict common at the centre, or was this a one-time incident? - **[TW]** 種姓衝突在中心常見嗎，還是只有這一次？ - **[EN]** How did the local Buddhists feel about having their own centre in this area? - **[TW]** 當地佛教徒對在這個地區有自己的中心有什麼感覺？

🔴 書稿對應：CH08 L774-790。這段的意義不在「苦」，而在種姓如何滲入看起來與世隔絕的空間。

Q6. 雨季和九月——這裡最美的時候

[EN] Lokamitra wrote that September was the most enjoyable time — the rains receding, the air fresh, the fields and forest a medley of different greens, and they would bathe in the fast-flowing streams. Does that still hold true? When is your favourite time here?

[TW] 世友寫九月是最愉快的時候——雨漸停，空氣清新，田野和森林是各種層次的綠，他們會到湍急的溪裡洗澡。現在還是這樣嗎？您最喜歡這裡的什麼時候？

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** He also described the monsoon — waterfalls gushing down from the hills, fields becoming one continuous sheet of water. Did the centre ever flood? - **[TW]** 他也描寫了雨季——瀑布從山丘傾瀉而下，田地變成一片連續的水面。中心曾經淹水嗎？ - **[EN]** What about the animals? He mentioned snakes, wild pigs, and panthers behind the hills. Are they still around? - **[TW]** 動物呢？他提到山後有蛇、野豬和黑豹。現在還有嗎？

主題三：Bhaja 石窟——兩千年的連結

Q7. 世友在 Bhaja 的靈性體驗

[EN] Lokamitra told me that his deepest spiritual experiences happened here, in Bhaja — not at Ajanta. He was very clear about this. He said, "I had had those deep experiences in Bhaja a year earlier." What do you know about his connection with these caves?

💡 追問 / Follow-up Questions - **[EN]** Did he ever describe what happened to him here? Not in detail — just the feeling or the sense of it. - **[TW]** 他有沒有描述過在這裡發生了什麼？不需要細節——只是感覺或氛圍。 - **[EN]** He first visited in October 1978, before the centre existed. Do you know what drew him to these caves specifically? - **[TW]** 他 1978 年 10 月第一次來，那時中心還不存在。您知道是什麼把他吸引到這些石窟來的嗎？ - **[EN]** How does Bhaja feel different from Karla or Ajanta to you? - **[TW]** 對您來說，巴賈和卡爾拉或阿姜塔有什麼不同的感覺？

What does it look like? - **[TW]** 主窟的佛塔在夕陽照進來時會亮起來。您見過嗎？是什麼樣子？ - **[EN]** Do retreatants still go up to the caves to meditate? Or is it mainly for visiting now? - **[TW]** 靜修者現在還會上來石窟打坐嗎？還是主要是來參觀？
