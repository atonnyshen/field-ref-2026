Day 3（6/2）訪談作戰包

今日策略改變 原主行程把 6/2 放在 Kondhanpur / Vishaka 訪談；今天現場改版為：上午 Ambedkar Museum 與 Dapodi slum，下午仍理解 Jambudvipa Trust 與 Manuski 的組織細節，並到 Lokamitra 家中拜訪、訪問 Vishaka。
這不是偏離，而是更好的排列：先看安貝卡公共記憶與 Dapodi 城市現場，再回到機構史與家庭見證，今天可以把「思想、街區、組織、家庭」四條線扣在一起。

公開頁提醒 本檔會進 GitHub Pages noindex 公開版。請不要填入住址、電話、私人家庭照片細節、未獲同意的家庭成員資料。敏感內容用「本機筆記另記」。

二、上午 A：Ambedkar Museum

2.1 現場必看

  • 館名正式英文／馬拉地文名稱、入口標語、是否有 Dr. B. R. Ambedkar 全名。
  • 展示的安貝卡形象：憲法之父、達利特領袖、佛教皈依者、社會改革者、教育者，哪一層最突出？
  • 是否展示 1956 年皈依大典、二十二條誓願、《佛陀與其教法》、憲法、教育經歷、法律訓練、Mahad / Poona Pact / Yeola 等事件。
  • 觀眾如何觀看：學生、家庭、佛教徒、一般遊客；是否有人行禮、拍照、讀展板、帶小孩講解。
  • 色彩與物件：藍色、法輪、佛像、安貝卡肖像、西裝、書、筆、憲法、眼鏡、手勢。
  • 展板用語：是否使用 Dalit、Buddhist、Navayana、Equality、Liberty、Fraternity、Educate / Agitate / Organize。
  • 允許拍照時：每張展板先拍全景，再拍文字細節；若不允許，就用筆記記下關鍵詞。

2.2 開口問法（對導覽者／工作人員）

  1. "Which part of Dr. Ambedkar's life do visitors here usually respond to most strongly?"
  2. "Do Buddhist visitors and non-Buddhist visitors look at this museum differently?"
  3. "How do school groups usually learn about Dr. Ambedkar here?"
  4. "Is the 1956 conversion ceremony presented mainly as a religious event, a social event, or both?"
  5. "Are there any labels, booklets, or archival materials I can photograph or get a copy of?"

2.3 晚上可問 Lokamitra

  • "When you first came to Maharashtra, how did you gradually understand what Dr. Ambedkar meant to people here?"
  • "When you see how Dr. Ambedkar is remembered in a museum, what feels accurate to you, and what feels incomplete?"
  • "For readers in Taiwan who know very little about him, what should they understand first: the Constitution, caste, conversion, or the Dhamma?"

2.4 寫作判斷

Ambedkar Museum 不是為了補百科。它的價值是讓你看見：安貝卡不是章節裡的背景人物，而是公共記憶、教育制度、佛教身份與城市空間共同塑造出的精神源頭。正式書寫時，Museum 可作為 CH05 的「知識重量」入口，也可作為 CH12「安貝卡的佛教能給佛教世界什麼」的物件化線索。

三、上午 B：Dapodi slum / basti

今天的底線：不要把 Dapodi 寫成貧困奇觀。今天要看的是：Jeevak、Mahavihara、Dapodi slum、Jai Bhim 地景如何彼此相鄰，一個佛教運動如何在教育、健康、住房、排水、公共記憶與日常交通中找到可以有效介入的位置。

3.1 現場必看

  • 地名正式拼法：Dapodi / Bopodi / local basti 名稱是否需要消歧義。
  • 與 Mahavihara / Jeevak / Manuski / Jai Bhim Nagar 的實際距離：步行多久、車程多久。
  • 建材與變化：粗磚、鐵皮、塑膠布、加高樓層、水泥房、排水溝、雨季水痕。
  • 日常設施：學校、幼兒園、補習班、藥局、小店、佛堂、社區中心、政府服務點。
  • 公共記憶：Ambedkar 肖像、Jai Bhim、藍色旗幟、佛像、Phule / Shahu / Kabir / Bhagat Singh 等標誌。
  • 基礎設施：水、排水、廁所、電線、垃圾、道路、照明、手機網路。
  • 居民的自我稱呼：slum / basti / locality / nagar；不要把外來者詞彙硬套進去。
  • 兒童與青年：是否可見上學、補習、考公、職訓、女生教育與家庭照護的線索。

3.2 開口問法（英文）

  1. "What is the local name of this area, and how should I spell it?"
  2. "How has this area changed in the last twenty or thirty years?"
  3. "Did people improve their houses here, or were some families moved elsewhere?"
  4. "What changed first: water, drainage, electricity, school access, roads, or work opportunities?"
  5. "Which Buddhist or Ambedkarite centres are important for people here now?"
  6. "When Jeevak or Mahavihara worked with people here, what could they actually do effectively?"
  7. "What problems could only be handled by government or town planning?"

3.3 不要這樣問

避免問法 改成
"Is this a terrible slum?" "What are the actual living conditions here, and what has changed?"
"Did Lokamitra help these poor people?" "Which institutions or people were involved, and what did each do?"
"How did you escape this place?" "Do families tend to stay, improve their homes, or move elsewhere?"
"Were people helpless?" "What were people already doing for themselves before any project came in?"

3.4 寫作判斷

Day2 的 Jeevak 觀察已經顯示：教育與健康不是抽象慈善，而是讓家庭能往上移動的基礎設施。Dapodi slum 今天要補的是「空間壓力」：房子、排水、學校、街道、佛堂、小店、考公牆如何形成一個人實際生活的世界。正式書寫時，要把「苦」寫成具體條件，不把人寫成被觀看的苦難。

四、下午 C：Jambudvipa Trust 與 Manuski

4.1 今天要釐清的總問題

核心句：Jambudvipa / Manuski 不是 Lokamitra 另起爐灶，而是他在 Mahavihara、Jeevak、Nagaloka 經驗之後的「有機延續」。今天的任務，是把這個延續的法律、組織、空間、課程與人員結構問清楚。

4.2 必問：正式名稱與時序

  1. "What are the official legal names of Jambudvipa Trust and Manuski? Are they separate legal entities, or is Manuski a project under Jambudvipa?"
  2. "In which year was Jambudvipa Trust registered? In which year did Manuski begin?"
  3. "What was the first office or working space? Was it the small flat mentioned in the manuscript?"
  4. "Who were the first trustees, staff, or core assistants?"
  5. "How did the work move from Dharma classes and seminars into social action, training, and the Manuski network?"
  6. "What is the correct way to describe Manuski in the book: project, centre, network, fellowship, foundation, or something else?"

4.3 必問：Jambudvipa 的定位

  • "Jambudvipa is a name from Buddhist texts for the Indian subcontinent. How did Lokamitra understand that name?"
  • "Was Jambudvipa mainly about Dharma practice, Buddhist education, seminars, or the relationship between Dharma and society?"
  • "How was it connected with Nagaloka from the beginning?"
  • "What could be done through Jambudvipa that could not be done while Lokamitra was formally president of the earlier centres?"

4.4 必問：Manuski 的定位

  • "What does 'Manuski' mean in Marathi usage, and how is it connected with Dr. Ambedkar?"
  • "What were the first 25 Dalit-based NGOs or activists like? What made the network new?"
  • "What kinds of training did Manuski actually provide: capacity building, women's leadership, legal awareness, social action, government regulations, fellowship training?"
  • "What did Manuski do in atrocity cases or urgent social situations? Which examples can be publicly mentioned?"
  • "How do you avoid making Manuski sound like charity from outside, rather than Dalit leadership building itself?"

4.5 材料索取清單

  • Jambudvipa Trust / Manuski 官方名稱、註冊年份、簡介或章程。
  • 一頁式組織年表：Mahavihara → Jambudvipa → Manuski → Nagaloka / alumni programs。
  • 年報、簡報、brochure、課程表、training manual、Manuski Fellowship 文件。
  • 早期小公寓／第一個辦公室／現在 Manuski Centre 的照片或可公開描述。
  • Manuski Network 初期 25 個 NGO / activists 的名單或類型說明。
  • 可公開提及的案例清單：Kherlanji、Gujarat、COVID、fellowship、women's leadership。
  • 哪些內容不可公開、不可寫入中文書稿。

4.6 寫作判斷

Jambudvipa 要寫成「退到小處後反而看見大局」：從正式中心會長的角色退下，轉入較低調、較自由、較能連接學者、社運者、達利特與部落組織的空間。Manuski 則是這個空間裡更靠近社會行動的一支。不要把它寫成 NGO 成功學；要寫成佛法、安貝卡思想、城市社群與本地領導力訓練交會出的平台。

五、下午/晚間 D：Lokamitra 家訪

5.1 進門前提醒

  • 先問哪些地方可以拍照、哪些完全不拍。
  • 不記住址、不拍門牌、不公開家庭成員私人資訊。
  • 家中物件若要寫：先問「這可以寫嗎？」
  • 家訪不是觀光；重點是理解生活如何承載長期工作。

5.2 現場必看

  • 書、照片、文件、佛像、安貝卡相關物件、台灣相關物件，哪些是日常擺放。
  • 工作與生活的邊界：是否有書桌、檔案、訪客空間、家庭空間。
  • Vishaka 的動線與角色：她如何參與接待、家務、宗教或組織記憶。
  • Lokamitra 身體狀態與訪談節奏：不要超時；累了就停。

5.3 可問 Lokamitra

  1. "Which parts of your home life are private and should not be described in the book?"
  2. "Are there any objects here that help explain your life in India, and that you are comfortable with me writing about?"
  3. "When you moved from the Mahavihara office to the smaller Jambudvipa setting, what changed in your daily rhythm?"
  4. "How did your family life and the movement's work support or complicate each other?"
  5. "How would you like Vishaka's role to be represented, without putting words into her mind?"

六、Vishaka 訪談（最重要）

定位：Vishaka 不是「補充 Lokamitra 故事的配角」。她是獨立見證者。今天不是把她拉進既有敘事裡，而是讓她自己劃定：哪些可以說、哪些被誤解、哪些不應寫。

6.1 開場同意式問法

先用英文短句，讓她知道她可以停：

"I know most of the written material comes from Lokamitra's perspective. I don't want to put thoughts into your mind. I would be grateful to hear what you feel is important, and you can skip any question."

再補：

"Some things may be private or painful. If anything should not be recorded or written, please just tell me."

6.2 名字與身份線

  1. "How would you like me to refer to you in the book: Ranjana, Vishaka, or both in different periods?"
  2. "When did you receive the Dharma name Vishaka? Is there a year or ceremony you remember?"
  3. "Was there a moment when you felt the movement had become your own path, not only Lokamitra's work?"
  4. "Is there anything about the word 'Dharmacharini' that Chinese readers should understand?"

6.3 家庭與婚姻線（CH05）

  1. "What do you remember about first meeting Lokamitra and the Dharma classes in Ambedkar Society?"
  2. "Your father supported the marriage. What did Dr. Ambedkar and Buddhism mean in your family?"
  3. "Were there concerns from neighbours or relatives? What were those concerns actually about?"
  4. "What kind of support mattered most in the early years: family, Dharma friends, practical help, or something else?"
  5. "Are there common misunderstandings about your marriage that you would like the book to avoid?"

6.4 家庭生活與看不見的工作

  1. "What kind of work did you do that may not appear in Lokamitra's manuscript?"
  2. "How did family life work alongside the centres, trusts, visitors, retreats, and travel?"
  3. "When the children were young, what was most difficult practically?"
  4. "What did you want the children to understand about the work?"
  5. "Are there moments of ordinary daily life that you think explain the movement better than big public events?"

6.5 國籍時間線（CH09 高優先）

這題很重要，但要輕輕問，因為它是法律時間線，不是身份審問。

  1. "For the book's timeline, may I ask when you became an Indian citizen?"
  2. "Is there a year, document, or family memory that can anchor it?"
  3. "Before that year, were there practical difficulties because of citizenship or legal status?"
  4. "What is safe for us to state publicly?"

6.6 Kondhanpur / 2001 事件（只在她願意時問）

先問同意： "There is one difficult event in Kondhanpur. I only want to ask if you are comfortable. We can also leave it for another day."

若她願意：

  1. "What do you remember as the important facts of that day?"
  2. "What should the book not exaggerate or misunderstand?"
  3. "The manuscript says you refused to leave until the matter was sorted out. How would you describe your decision?"
  4. "Who decided not to pursue charges, and why? What should be said publicly, and what should remain private?"
  5. "Did that event change how you understood the work, the law, or caste in village areas?"

6.7 收尾：她希望書怎麼寫

  1. "If readers in Taiwan know you only through this book, what would you most want them to understand?"
  2. "What should I be careful not to romanticize?"
  3. "Is there anything about Indian Buddhist women, family life, or the movement that outsiders often miss?"
  4. "Would you be willing to review the paragraphs about you later, to make sure they are fair?"

6.8 寫作判斷

Vishaka 訪談的最大價值不是「增加情節」，而是糾正敘事權力。既有素材多由 Lokamitra 外部觀察而來；今天如果能取得她自己的說法，CH05 婚姻、CH09 家庭與國籍、CH11 Kondhanpur 都要改成更謹慎的雙視角：事件可以有事實，情緒必須由她自己給詞。

七、今日紅線備忘

  • 不使用高衝突動詞與英雄化語彙；所有工作寫成團隊、制度、社群、家族與本地人的共同結果。
  • 不把貧困寫成「地獄畫面」；只寫具體條件與具體改變。
  • 不替 Vishaka 代寫心情；她若沉默，就尊重沉默。
  • 不把 Jambudvipa / Manuski 寫成西方人創辦的慈善故事；核心是本地 Ambedkarite / Dalit leadership。
  • 不把安貝卡 Museum 寫成百科館；它是公共記憶現場。
  • 不公開家庭住址、電話、私人照片、未獲同意的家庭資訊。

九、口袋版：今天十個最重要問題

  1. Ambedkar Museum 最強調安貝卡哪一面：憲法、種姓、佛教，還是教育？
  2. Dapodi 這個地方，當地人自己怎麼稱呼？
  3. Dapodi 近二十到三十年最實際的改變是什麼？
  4. Jeevak / Mahavihara / Manuski 與 Dapodi 居民的日常距離有多近？
  5. Jambudvipa Trust 的正式註冊年份與第一個辦公空間是什麼？
  6. Manuski 是 project、network、centre、foundation，還是 fellowship？正式稱呼怎麼寫？
  7. Jambudvipa / Manuski 如何被理解為 Mahavihara / Nagaloka 的有機延續？
  8. Vishaka 希望書中稱她 Ranjana、Vishaka，還是依時期分開？
  9. Vishaka 成為印度公民的確切年份或記憶錨點是什麼？
  10. 今天哪些家庭與組織細節可以公開，哪些必須只留本機筆記？

十、今日最重要的句子

今天的核心不是問完所有題，而是讓四條線彼此照亮：安貝卡如何成為公共記憶，Dapodi 如何成為工作現場，Jambudvipa / Manuski 如何成為制度延續，Vishaka 如何把家庭與運動的真實邊界說回她自己手裡。
