一、Social Work As Spiritual Practice

  1. 中：早期 Jeevak／Dapodi 的健康與教育工作，手稿中被描述為佛法修行的表達、也是 “Dharma in action”。以您的經驗，應該如何精確理解 “social work as spiritual practice”？它和一般 charity 或 social service 最大的差別是什麼？ EN: In the early Jeevak and Dapodi health-and-education work, the manuscripts describe social projects as an expression of Dharma practice and “Dharma in action.” From your experience, how should we understand “social work as spiritual practice” precisely? How is it different from ordinary charity or social service?

  2. 中：如果一位健康工作者教母親用糖鹽水處理腹瀉，或宿舍替女孩付回校車票，佛法修行究竟在哪裡發生：在慈悲、正命、戒律、共同工作、還是改變社會結構之中？ EN: If a health worker teaches a mother how to use sugar-salt water for diarrhoea, or a hostel pays for a girl’s ticket back to school, where exactly is the Dharma practice happening: in compassion, Right Livelihood, ethics, collective work, or social transformation?

  3. 中：您 23 歲辭去政府工作，成為 full-time worker。回頭看，這對您來說是工作選擇、信仰選擇，還是一種修行選擇？哪一件日常困難最讓您感覺「工作本身在訓練我」？ EN: You left a secure government job at 23 to become a full-time worker. Looking back, was that a career choice, a faith choice, or a spiritual-practice choice? Which daily difficulty most made you feel that the work itself was training you?

二、女生宿舍與可公開匿名案例

  1. 中：暑假返家後，有些女孩可能被親戚阻止返校、被要求早婚、做家務或工作，甚至只是沒有車票錢。能否提供兩到三個可公開、去識別化的真實案例？哪些細節必須遮蔽，哪些可以寫入書中？ EN: After returning home for summer holidays, some girls may be prevented from coming back, pressured into early marriage, domestic work, paid work, or simply lack ticket money. Could you share two or three real cases that can be anonymized and publicly used? Which details must be hidden, and which may be included in the book?

  2. 中：當女孩放假返鄉時，宿舍或團隊如何降低她們失學的風險？例如家長會、學校協調、電話追蹤、車票支援、日用品或捐款管理，實際流程是什麼？ EN: When girls return home for holidays, how does the hostel or team reduce the risk of dropout? What is the actual process: parent meetings, school coordination, phone follow-up, ticket support, daily necessities, or donation management?

  3. 中：能否找一個生命路徑完整的案例：原本可能失學或早婚，後來完成教育、工作獨立，甚至回頭支持宿舍或下一代女孩？ EN: Can we identify one complete life-story case: a girl who might have dropped out or married early, but later completed her education, became independent, and perhaps returned to support the hostel or the next generation?

三、WSP 的時間線與防護網定位

  1. 中：我們目前聽到 WSP 從 2017 年開始，但 Day6 訪談又提到今年 4 月進入新 phase，之前也曾做 3-5 歲幼兒、青春期女孩、婦女 livelihood、domestic violence、POCSO、Maitri Network。請您幫我們校正：WSP 的正確發展時間線是什麼？ EN: We heard that WSP began in 2017, but the Day 6 interview also mentioned a new phase starting this April, and earlier work with 3-5-year-old children, adolescent girls, women’s livelihoods, domestic violence, POCSO, and the Maitri Network. Could you help correct the timeline of WSP’s development?

  2. 中：把 WSP 寫成「從婦女工作擴展為涵蓋幼兒、青春期女孩、男孩、婦女、家庭與司法轉介的社區防護網」，這個說法準確嗎？如果不準確，您會怎麼改寫？ EN: Is it accurate to describe WSP as having expanded from women’s work into a community protection network covering young children, adolescent girls, boys, women, families, and legal referrals? If not, how would you correct that wording?

  3. 中：WSP、Girls Hostel、Maitri Network、One-Stop Centre、police station、CWC 之間，實際上如何分工？哪些是 TBMSG 自己做，哪些是政府或外部網絡轉介？ EN: How do WSP, the Girls’ Hostel, the Maitri Network, the One-Stop Centre, police stations, and the CWC actually divide responsibilities? Which parts are done by TBMSG itself, and which are referrals to government or external networks?

四、Jambudvipa 與 Nagaloka 的關係

  1. 中：Day4 筆記裡出現「Nagaloka 是 Jambudvipa 的 satellite」這個說法。這是您原本的意思嗎？它準確嗎？還是應該改成 close association、training arm、network node、operational base，或另一種說法？ EN: In the Day 4 notes, Nagaloka is described as a “satellite” of Jambudvipa. Was that your intended meaning? Is it accurate? Or should we use another term, such as close association, training arm, network node, operational base, or something else?

  2. 中：1998 世友離開大精舍正式職責、1999 成立 Jambudvipa、2002 Nagaloka residential training 開始，這三者之間的實際關係是什麼？Jambudvipa 對 Nagaloka 提供的是法律平台、行政、募款、課程設計、人員，還是精神方向？ EN: What was the practical relationship among Lokamitra leaving formal responsibility at the Mahavihara in 1998, founding Jambudvipa in 1999, and the Nagaloka residential training beginning in 2002? Did Jambudvipa provide a legal platform, administration, fundraising, curriculum design, personnel, spiritual direction, or something else?

  3. 中：為什麼世友需要 Jambudvipa 這個新平台？哪些工作是在原來 Mahavihara／TBMSG 架構下不容易做、但透過 Jambudvipa 可以做的？ EN: Why did Lokamitra need Jambudvipa as a new platform? What kinds of work were difficult within the earlier Mahavihara or TBMSG structure, but became possible through Jambudvipa?

五、Jeevak 與 Pune University／志工合作

  1. 中：Jeevak 或 TBMSG 是否曾與 Pune University、social work departments、NSS、college volunteers 或 medical students 有正式合作？如果有，請問是哪一年、哪個系所或 college、合作內容是什麼？ EN: Did Jeevak or TBMSG ever have formal collaborations with Pune University, social-work departments, NSS, college volunteers, or medical students? If yes, in which years, with which departments or colleges, and what did the collaboration involve?

  2. 中：如果沒有正式合作，study class 的老師主要來自哪裡：社區裡長大的學生、已上 college 的校友、外部志工，還是全職工作者？這個「受助者回來教下一代」的循環是否能找到一位可訪談人物？ EN: If there was no formal collaboration, where did study-class teachers mainly come from: students who grew up in the community, alumni who had gone to college, outside volunteers, or full-time workers? Can we identify one interviewee who embodies the cycle of “former beneficiary returning to teach the next generation”?

  3. 中：Pune University 離 Dapodi 社區並不遠，但對 slum 孩子來說可能是另一個世界。您記得孩子們如何看待那所大學嗎？有沒有人真的從 study class、hostel 或 Jeevak 系統一路走到大學、政府工作或專業職業？ EN: Pune University is physically close to Dapodi, but for children in the slum it may have felt like another world. How did the children understand that university? Did anyone move from the study class, hostel, or Jeevak system into university, government work, or a professional career?
